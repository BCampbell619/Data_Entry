Active to Active Moves

Systematic Processes

FOCUS: This document explains the systematic processes of creating a label for each SKU that is being moved from one active to another. The menus that are associated with each process are also depicted with this document. Furthermore, any policies that are associated with this process will be explained as well.

NOTICE: Active to active moves can occur for a variety of reasons. The following are examples of why active to active moves may occur:

  • Projects
  • Product Alignment
  • Correct Bad Put Aways
  • Free Up Locations for Put Away

The important thing to understand is that all Active to Active moves will need approval before being put together. Supervision will either come to the data entry clerk with a move/project or, the data entry clerk will need to get approval from supervision to make moves.

The Active to Active Sheet

Overview

Active to active moves are made up on an Excel Spreadsheet template such as in the example below:

The spreadsheet handles the following attributes (click attribute to view description):

  • SKU

    • The SKU column handles the SKU numbers that are moving from one location to another

  • DESCRIPTION

    • The Description of the product being moved is placed in the description column of the sheet

  • FROM

    • The ‘FROM’ column handles the location that the product is moving from (i.e. the old location)

  • QTY

    • The QTY column takes the SKU's current inventory that is in its current active location (the ‘FROM’ location)

  • TO

    • The ‘TO’ column handles the ‘new’ location to which the SKU is being moved

  • PICKING

    • The Picking column is an important column. There are two options that can be put in this column – ‘Y’ or ‘N’ The significance of whether a SKU is picking or not will be explained in more detail below.

Picking Vs. Non–Picking

PICKING – When an item is picking there has to be some coordination done between the Forklift Department and the Orderfilling Department before it can be moved systematically.


Here are the steps to follow when communicating a Picking move:


  • Make up Active to Active Sheet
  • Make up & print the labels for Forklift
  • Update MIN/MAX's for the ‘To’ locations
  • Email moves to Orderfilling, Forklift, & Upper Management
    • Signify within the email where the moves are taking place and that they're picking
    • Signify the purpose of the moves
    • Signify that the moves will not be keyed in until Orderfilling or Stock gives clearance to do so
    • Signify that the MIN/MAX's have been updated

Once the Orderfilling or Forklift Supervisor responds to the email stating that the moves can be ‘keyed in’ it is safe to systematically move the SKUs.


NON–PICKING –When an item is not picking then the process of systematically moving an item can move more quickly.


Here are the steps for non–picking moves:


  • Make up Active to Active Sheet
  • Make up and print labels for Forklift
  • Systematically move the SKUs to the ‘To’ locations
  • Update MIN/MAX's for the ‘To’ locations
  • Email moves to Orderfilling, Forklift, & Upper Management
    • Signify within the email where the moves are taking place and that they're non–picking
    • Signify the purpose of the moves
    • Signify that the moves have been keyed in
    • Signify that the MIN/MAX's have been updated

NOTE: There is a time during the day shift that one should be cautious when creating active to active non–picking moves . At approximately 11:30AM the new wave schedule starts to build. When this occurs a non–picking item can go from non–picking to picking. When doing active to active moves around this time frame it is best to wait until the waves are done building before proceeding.


It is still good practice to ask for an email responce from the Forklift department to signify that the moves have been completed. This provides a way of tracking the completion of moves for everyone involved – especially when there are several sets of non–picking moves.

Case Label Creation & Print

Creation Process

Within the ‘Putaway Specialist Menu’ under the submenu heading ‘Misc’ is a menu option titled ‘ RF Inbound Menu.’

Within the ‘RF Inbound Menu’ is another menu option titled ‘Pack Cs Fr Actv’ which stands for Pack Case From Active.

The first step in the label creation process is to create the case label number. The pattern of the case lable number is a leading four zeros followed by the date (YYYYMMDD), which is followed by the SKU of the item.

After the case label number has been entered in the case field you will be prompted for a SKU. Enter the SKU to proceed to the next step.

The last step is to assign a single unit to the case. Enter ‘1’ in the QTY field and then confirm the case.

This process will be repeated for however many SKUs are on the active to active move sheet template.
