Routing

How to Open a Route

FOCUS:This guide will take you step by step through the process of attaching a route to a shipping dock door.

Select the menu option titled ‘Work With Routes – rtl’ and this ‘From’ and ‘To’ screen will appear:


When the ‘From’ and ‘To’ screen is reached you will need to enter your route number. The term ‘Route’ is synonymous with ‘Store,’ which can be found on your wave sheet. You can enter the route number in just the ‘From’ field, or it can be placed in both the ‘From’ and ‘To’ fields. After you have entered the route press ‘Enter’.

Once the desired route (i.e. the store number) is entered the next screen should display the route in an ‘unassigned’ status as shown below:


The next step is to activate the route. This is done by taking an option ‘2’ in the cursor field to the left of the route number and pressing ‘Enter’.

The ‘Activate Route’ option is the first option on this screen:


The next screen will have a few empty fields. These are the fields used to assign a route to a dock door.


There are two pieces of information that you need to concern yourself with, are the following:


  • Dock/Door
  • Trailer

The first empty field of ‘Dock/Door’ will always need the same four letters – ‘DOOR’. The second empty field will change depending on the door that is being assigned to the route. It can be from ‘N102’ to ‘N125’. This will depend mostly on what the Wave Sheet dictates.


Trailer is fairly consistent as well. The first empty field will always require ‘BIG5’ and the second empty field always takes the route number.


Once all the fields have been completed press ‘Enter’ and then ‘F16’.

This is a depiction of a completed route activiation:


Once the route has been confirmed you should be taken back to the main route screen. Press ‘F5’ to refresh the screen and ensure the route shows a status of ‘Activated’.


Once this is acheived you have successfully activated a route. Be sure to mark your wave sheet of the changes made to the route, and realize that the wave associated with the route is now eligible for release.
